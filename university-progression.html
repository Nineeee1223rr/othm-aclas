University Progression

Your OTHM diploma is a stepping stone to a full university degree. Gain advanced entry to bachelor's and master's programmes worldwide.

The Progression Route

OTHM qualifications are mapped to the UK Regulated Qualifications Framework (RQF), meaning universities recognise their academic level and credit value.

If You Complete...You Can Progress To...
Level 3 DiplomaYear 1 of a UK bachelor's degree
Level 4 + 5 Diploma (240 credits)Final year (top-up) of a UK bachelor's degree
Level 6 Diploma (120 credits)Final year (top-up) or entry to postgraduate study
Level 7 Diploma (120 credits)MBA or Master's degree top-up (dissertation stage)

What Is a Top-Up Degree?

A top-up degree is the final stage of a university programme. If you hold an OTHM Level 4+5 or Level 6 diploma, many universities grant advanced entry - you join in the final year. In most cases, this takes just one academic year. At the end, you graduate with a full bachelor's or master's degree from that university.

Example Pathway

1. You complete an OTHM Level 4+5 Diploma in Business Management (240 credits) with ACLAS Global. 2. You apply to a UK university for a BA (Hons) Business Management (Top-Up) - final year entry. 3. You complete one year of study, online or on campus. 4. You graduate with a full bachelor's degree.

Where Can You Progress?

OTHM diplomas are accepted by universities across the UK and internationally. Progression is always at the discretion of the receiving institution. Here are some universities that have accepted OTHM qualifications:

UK Universities

Anglia Ruskin University, University of Sunderland, University of Northampton, UCLan, University of Derby, Coventry University, Arden University, BPP University, and many more.

USA & Canada

OTHM certificates can be verified through World Education Services (WES) for $50. Accepted for credit transfer and graduate programme entry at selected institutions.

Australia & Worldwide

Learners have progressed to universities in Australia, New Zealand, Singapore, Malaysia, UAE, and across Europe.
