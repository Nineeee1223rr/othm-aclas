## 1. Purpose

This policy sets out the Centre's commitment to the continuing professional development of all staff who deliver, assess, or quality-assure OTHM qualifications. CPD ensures that the Centre's staff maintain and develop the subject knowledge, pedagogical skills, and assessment competence needed to provide a high-quality learner experience.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all Tutors, Assessors, and Internal Quality Assurers.

## 3. CPD Expectations

Every Tutor, Assessor, and IQA is expected to:

- Engage in CPD activities relevant to their role for a minimum of 30 hours per academic year.
- Maintain a personal CPD log recording all activities.
- Apply learning from CPD to their practice.
- Discuss their CPD progress with the Centre Quality Lead at least twice per year.

## 4. What Counts as CPD?

The Centre recognises a broad range of activities:

### Formal
- Accredited courses and qualifications (including Assessor and IQA qualifications)
- OTHM webinars, briefings, and standardisation events
- Conferences and professional workshops
- Online courses from recognised providers

### Informal
- Peer observation of teaching or assessment, with reflective discussion
- Participation in the Centre's standardisation meetings
- Reading and research in the relevant subject area
- Reflective practice (documented)
- Mentoring or being mentored
- Review of EQA reports and action planning
- Developing or revising teaching and assessment materials

## 5. Individual Development Plan (IDP)

Each staff member maintains an Individual Development Plan, reviewed with the Centre Quality Lead. The IDP identifies:

- Current strengths
- Areas for development
- Specific CPD objectives for the coming year
- Planned activities and timelines
- How the learning will be applied

## 6. Centre Support

The Centre supports staff CPD by:

- Providing funding for approved CPD activities, within the annual CPD budget
- Allocating time within working hours for CPD
- Organising internal standardisation meetings and training sessions
- Providing access to online CPD resources and OTHM guidance materials
- Recognising CPD achievement in performance reviews

## 7. CPD Records

Each staff member maintains a CPD log recording:

- Date and duration of the activity
- Description
- Learning outcomes or reflections
- Evidence (certificate, attendance record, notes)
- How the learning has been applied

CPD logs are available for inspection by the Centre Quality Lead and by OTHM/EQAs upon request.

## 8. CPD and Quality Improvement

CPD needs identified through IQA sampling, EQA feedback, or learner feedback are fed into the Centre's quality improvement planning. Relevant CPD activities are prioritised.
