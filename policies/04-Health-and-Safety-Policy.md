## 1. Policy Statement

ACLAS Global International Education Group is committed to providing a safe and healthy environment for all learners, staff, contractors, and visitors. This commitment extends to the Centre's physical premises, its online learning environment, and any off-site activities it organises. The Centre recognises its duty of care under applicable health and safety legislation, including the Health and Safety at Work etc. Act 1974 (where applicable to UK-based operations), relevant US federal OSHA standards, and Georgia state regulations.

The Centre believes that a safe environment is foundational to effective learning and that health and safety is the responsibility of every person associated with the Centre.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:

- All employees, contractors, and volunteers working for or on behalf of the Centre
- All learners enrolled on OTHM qualifications, whether studying online, through blended learning, or attending face-to-face sessions
- Any visitors to the Centre's physical premises
- Any third parties engaged by the Centre (e.g., IT support providers, facility maintenance)

## 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **DSE (Display Screen Equipment)** | Any computer, laptop, tablet, or other device with a display screen used for work or study, as defined under the Health and Safety (Display Screen Equipment) Regulations 1992 |
| **DSE Assessment** | An evaluation of a user's workstation, posture, screen use, and working environment to identify and mitigate risks |
| **Risk Assessment** | A systematic process of evaluating potential risks that may be involved in a projected activity or undertaking |
| **Competent Person** | A person who has sufficient training, experience, and knowledge to assist in undertaking health and safety measures |
| **Near Miss** | An unplanned event that did not result in injury, illness, or damage but had the potential to do so |
| **Remote Working** | Working or studying from a location other than the Centre's principal office |

## 4. Responsibilities

### 4.1 Centre Director

The Centre Director holds overall responsibility for health and safety, including:

- Ensuring adequate resources (time, budget, equipment) are allocated
- Appointing a competent person for health and safety
- Ensuring risk assessments are conducted and reviewed
- Approving and resourcing the Health and Safety Action Plan
- Reporting on health and safety to governance meetings
- Ensuring this policy is implemented, communicated, and reviewed

### 4.2 All Staff

Every staff member is responsible for:

- Taking reasonable care of their own health and safety and that of others who may be affected by their acts or omissions
- Cooperating with the Centre on health and safety matters
- Reporting hazards, incidents, and near-misses promptly
- Completing all mandatory health and safety training
- Following safe systems of work and using equipment correctly
- Not misusing or interfering with anything provided for health and safety

### 4.3 Learners

Learners are expected to:

- Follow any health and safety instructions provided
- Take reasonable care of their own health and safety while studying
- Report concerns promptly to their Tutor or the Centre Director
- Complete the DSE self-assessment checklist provided during induction
- Conduct themselves in a manner that does not endanger themselves or others

## 5. Physical Premises

The Centre's principal office is located at **8735 Dunwoody Place, Suite R, Atlanta, GA 30350, USA**.

### 5.1 Fire Safety

- A fire risk assessment is conducted annually and reviewed whenever there is a material change to the premises or its use.
- Emergency evacuation routes are clearly marked, kept unobstructed, and displayed on floor plans.
- Fire extinguishers are maintained and inspected annually.
- Fire drills are conducted at least annually for any staff working on the premises.
- All staff are trained on the emergency evacuation procedure.

### 5.2 First Aid

- Adequate first-aid supplies are maintained on the premises and inspected quarterly.
- At least one staff member holds a current first-aid qualification (where staffing levels permit).
- Emergency contact numbers (911 and local emergency services) are displayed prominently.

### 5.3 General Workplace Safety

- The premises comply with applicable Fulton County and Georgia State building and safety codes.
- Electrical equipment is visually inspected and PAT-tested (or equivalent) periodically.
- Slips, trips, and falls risks are identified and controlled.
- Adequate lighting, ventilation, and temperature control are maintained.
- The premises are cleaned regularly and waste is disposed of safely.

## 6. Display Screen Equipment (DSE)

Recognising that the majority of the Centre's staff and learners spend significant time using computers:

### 6.1 For Staff Working at the Centre's Office

- A DSE workstation assessment is completed for each staff member upon commencement and reviewed annually.
- Assessments cover: chair, desk, screen position, keyboard, mouse, lighting, and breaks.
- Where risks are identified, reasonable adjustments are made (e.g., ergonomic chair, wrist rest, monitor riser).

### 6.2 For Remote Staff and Learners

- A DSE self-assessment checklist is provided during induction.
- Guidance on setting up an ergonomic home study space is included in the Learner Handbook and Moodle induction module.
- The guidance covers:
  - Chair and desk height
  - Screen positioning (top of screen at or slightly below eye level)
  - Keyboard and mouse placement
  - Lighting and glare reduction
  - The importance of regular breaks (the 20-20-20 rule: every 20 minutes, look at something 20 feet away for 20 seconds)
  - Posture and stretching exercises

## 7. Mental Health and Wellbeing

The Centre recognises that mental health is an integral part of health and safety:

- Staff and learners are encouraged to discuss wellbeing concerns with a trusted member of staff.
- The Centre promotes a culture where mental health is discussed openly and without stigma.
- Learner engagement monitoring (see Blended and Distance Learning Policy) includes a wellbeing check when a learner becomes disengaged.
- Staff have access to support resources, including signposting to external mental health services.
- Reasonable adjustments may be made for learners and staff with mental health conditions (see Reasonable Adjustment Policy).

## 8. Online Safety

See the **Safeguarding Policy** for detailed provisions on online safety. In summary:

- All online interactions between staff and learners take place through Centre-approved channels.
- The Moodle platform is secured and monitored.
- Learners receive guidance on safe online behaviour during induction.
- The Centre has a contingency plan for significant online service disruptions.

## 9. Risk Assessment

The Centre conducts and documents risk assessments for:

| Area | Frequency | Reviewer |
|------|-----------|----------|
| Physical office premises | Annually and on material change | Centre Director / Competent Person |
| Fire safety | Annually | Centre Director |
| DSE workstations | On commencement and annually | Staff member (self-assessment) |
| Online delivery and data security | Annually | Centre Director / IT provider |
| Off-site activities (if any) | Before each activity | Organising staff member |

Each risk assessment:
- Identifies the hazards
- Evaluates the level of risk (likelihood × severity)
- Specifies control measures to eliminate or reduce the risk
- Identifies who is responsible for implementing controls
- Is reviewed after any incident or change in circumstances

## 10. Incident Reporting and Investigation

### 10.1 Reporting

1. All accidents, incidents, and near-misses are reported to the Centre Director immediately (within 24 hours for non-urgent matters).
2. The incident is recorded in the Centre's **Incident Log**, including: date, time, location, people involved, description, immediate actions taken, and any witnesses.
3. For serious incidents (requiring medical treatment, resulting in absence, or involving a significant risk), the Centre Director is notified immediately.

### 10.2 Investigation

- Serious incidents are investigated to identify root causes and preventive measures.
- The investigation report includes: what happened, why it happened, what has been done to prevent recurrence, and recommendations for further action.
- The report is reviewed by the Centre Director, and action points are tracked to completion.

### 10.3 External Reporting

Where legally required, incidents are reported to the relevant authorities:
- OSHA (for workplace incidents in the US meeting reporting thresholds)
- OTHM (where the incident affects learners, assessment, or quality assurance)
- Insurance provider (where a claim may arise)

## 11. Training and Communication

- Health and safety information is included in staff induction and learner induction.
- Any staff member with specific safety responsibilities (e.g., fire warden, first aider) receives appropriate training.
- Staff complete an annual health and safety refresher briefing.
- This policy is communicated to all staff and is available to learners on the Moodle platform and the Centre's website.
- Health and safety is a standing agenda item at quarterly governance meetings.

## 12. Monitoring

The Centre monitors:
- Number and type of incidents and near-misses reported
- Completion rates for DSE assessments and health and safety training
- Fire drill outcomes
- Status of risk assessment reviews
- Health and safety-related complaints
