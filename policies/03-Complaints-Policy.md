## 1. Purpose

This policy provides a clear, fair, confidential, and timely process for any person—learner, staff member, or member of the public—to raise a complaint about the Centre''s services, conduct, or operations. The Centre treats every complaint as an opportunity for service improvement and is committed to resolving concerns at the earliest possible stage.

## 2. Scope

This policy covers **non-academic complaints** about any aspect of the Centre''s operations, including but not limited to:

| Category | Examples |
|----------|----------|
| **Teaching and Learning Delivery** | Quality, timeliness, or availability of tutoring, learning resources, or course materials |
| **Staff Conduct or Behaviour** | Unprofessional behaviour, discrimination, harassment, or failure to follow Centre policy |
| **Administrative Services** | Errors or delays in enrolment, OTHM registration, certification, or fee processing |
| **Facilities and Technology** | Moodle platform availability, accessibility, technical issues, or office facilities |
| **Marketing and Information** | Misleading, inaccurate, or incomplete published information about qualifications, fees, or services |
| **Service Quality** | Any other aspect of the learner experience not addressed elsewhere |
| **Third-Party Services** | Complaints about recruitment agents, payment processors, or other partners acting on behalf of the Centre |

### What This Policy Does NOT Cover

- **Academic appeals** (disputes about assessment grades, RPL decisions, or reasonable adjustment decisions) — see the **Appeals Policy**.
- **Malpractice or plagiarism findings** — see the **Malpractice and Plagiarism Policy** (which includes its own appeals route).
- **Safeguarding concerns** — see the **Safeguarding Policy**.
- **Staff grievances** — covered by the Centre''s internal HR procedures, not this policy.

A complaint that raises both academic and non-academic issues is handled primarily under this policy, with the academic elements directed to the appropriate academic procedure.


## 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Complaint** | An expression of dissatisfaction about the Centre''s services, conduct, or operations that requires a response |
| **Complainant** | The person making the complaint |
| **Vexatious Complaint** | A complaint that is pursued in a manner that is obsessive, harassing, or disproportionate, even after the Centre''s process has been exhausted |
| **Service Recovery** | Actions taken by the Centre to restore the complainant''s confidence following a justified complaint |
| **Mediation** | A voluntary, facilitated conversation between the complainant and respondent, led by an impartial mediator, aimed at reaching a mutually acceptable resolution |

## 4. Principles

The Centre is committed to the following principles:

1. **Accessibility** — The complaints procedure is published on the Centre''s website and Moodle. Complaints may be submitted in any reasonable format. Reasonable adjustments are made to the procedure for complainants with additional needs.
2. **Fairness** — Every complaint is investigated on its merits by a person who is independent of the matter.
3. **Confidentiality** — Information about a complaint is shared only with those who need it to investigate or resolve the matter.
4. **No Retaliation** — The complainant and any witnesses suffer no disadvantage, victimisation, or retaliation for raising a complaint in good faith.
5. **Timeliness** — Complaints are acknowledged promptly and resolved within published timeframes.
6. **Continuous Improvement** — Complaint data is analysed to identify recurring issues and systemic weaknesses, feeding into the Quality Improvement Plan.

## 5. Classification of Complaints

| Level | Description | Handling Route |
|-------|-------------|----------------|
| **Level 1 — Minor** | An isolated error, delay, or communication failure with limited impact | Stage 1 — Informal Resolution |
| **Level 2 — Moderate** | A pattern of poor service, significant error, or conduct concern | Stage 2 — Formal Investigation |
| **Level 3 — Serious** | Allegations of discrimination, serious misconduct, systemic failure, or potential regulatory breach | Fast-tracked to Stage 2; Centre Director notified immediately; OTHM may be informed |

## 6. Stage 1 — Informal Resolution

The Centre encourages complainants to resolve concerns informally where possible.

1. The complainant raises the matter directly with the relevant staff member. If the complainant is uncomfortable approaching the staff member directly, they may contact the Centre Director.
2. The person receiving the complaint listens, acknowledges the concern, and aims to resolve it within **5 working days**.
3. The resolution and any agreed actions are confirmed in writing (email is sufficient).
4. If the complainant is satisfied, the matter is closed. A brief note is recorded in the Complaints Log.
5. If dissatisfied, or if the matter is not suitable for informal resolution, the complainant may proceed to Stage 2.

Complainants always have the right to bypass Stage 1 and proceed directly to Stage 2.

## 7. Stage 2 — Formal Investigation

### 7.1 Submission
The complainant submits a written complaint to the Centre Director (director@aclas.global), including:
- Full name and contact details
- A clear description of the issue with relevant dates, names, and supporting evidence
- The impact and the outcome sought
- Whether Stage 1 was attempted

If the complainant cannot submit in writing, the Centre Director arranges an alternative method (e.g., recorded telephone conversation).

### 7.2 Acknowledgment
Acknowledged within **3 working days**, confirming receipt, identifying the Investigating Officer, and providing an indicative timeline.

### 7.3 Investigation
The Investigating Officer conducts an impartial investigation including:
- Reviewing all relevant records (enrolment records, communication logs, Moodle activity logs, financial records)
- Interviewing the complainant (accompanied by a supporter if desired, not a legal representative)
- Interviewing the respondent and any relevant witnesses
- Seeking specialist advice where needed

All interviews are documented. Participants receive a written summary and the opportunity to confirm or correct it.

### 7.4 Outcome
Written outcome within **15 working days** (or 20 working days for complex complaints, with notification of extension). The outcome letter includes:
1. Summary of the complaint and investigation conducted
2. Findings on each element
3. Decision: Upheld / partially upheld / not upheld
4. Reasons for the decision
5. Remedial actions with timescales and responsible persons
6. Service recovery measures (e.g., apology, correction of error, goodwill gesture)
7. Learning points and any policy or procedure changes
8. Right of escalation to OTHM

## 8. Stage 3 — Internal Review

Dissatisfied complainants may request an internal review within **10 working days**. The review is conducted by a senior person not involved in the original investigation. The reviewer examines whether:
- The investigation was conducted fairly and in accordance with this policy
- All relevant evidence was considered
- Conclusions were reasonable based on the evidence
- Any new evidence has come to light

Decision issued within **10 working days**.

## 9. Stage 4 — Escalation to OTHM

The complainant may contact OTHM at info@othm.org.uk. The Centre cooperates fully with any OTHM investigation. OTHM''s jurisdiction is limited to matters relating to the delivery, assessment, or quality assurance of OTHM qualifications.

## 10. Anonymous Complaints

Anonymous complaints are accepted and investigated. However, the Centre cannot provide an outcome to the complainant, and investigation may be limited if further information is needed.

## 11. Vexatious Complaints

The Centre reserves the right to classify a complaint as vexatious where the complainant pursues the same complaint repeatedly after the process has been exhausted, makes complaints that are obsessive or disproportionate, or uses abusive language. The decision is made by the Centre Director and communicated in writing with reasons.

## 12. Complaints About Senior Staff

Where a complaint is about the Centre Director, it is directed to the Lead IQA who acts as Investigating Officer. If independence cannot be achieved, the complainant is advised of the right to escalate directly to OTHM.

## 13. Mediation

For complaints involving interpersonal conflict, the Centre may offer voluntary mediation. Both parties must agree. Mediation is confidential. If mediation does not resolve the complaint, the formal investigation proceeds.

## 14. Record Keeping

The Centre maintains a **Complaints Log** recording: complaint reference, date received, complainant details, classification, summary, investigating officer, outcome, remedial actions, learning points, and closure confirmation.

Records of all formal (Stage 2) complaints are retained for a minimum of **3 years**.

## 15. Monitoring and Reporting

- The Centre Director reviews the Complaints Log **quarterly** to identify trends.
- An **annual Complaints Report** summarises complaints by category, resolution rates, upheld rates, key themes, and actions taken.
- The annual report feeds into the annual Self-Assessment Report and Quality Improvement Plan.
