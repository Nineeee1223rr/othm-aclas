## 1. Policy Statement

ACLAS Global International Education Group is committed to safeguarding and promoting the welfare of all learners. The Centre recognises its particular responsibility towards learners under the age of 18 and adults at risk (vulnerable adults), and is committed to ensuring that they learn in a safe environment free from abuse, harm, neglect, or exploitation.

This policy reflects the principles of:

- The Children Act 1989 and 2004
- The Care Act 2014
- The Counter-Terrorism and Security Act 2015 (including the Prevent Duty)
- Working Together to Safeguard Children (HM Government, 2018)
- The Safeguarding Vulnerable Groups Act 2006
- Keeping Children Safe in Education (KCSIE) — as a standard of good practice
- Relevant US federal and Georgia state child protection legislation

The Centre believes that safeguarding is everyone's responsibility and that the welfare of the learner is paramount.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:

- All learners under the age of 18
- All vulnerable adults (adults at risk), defined as any person aged 18 or over who:
  - Has needs for care and support (whether or not the local authority is meeting any of those needs)
  - Is experiencing, or is at risk of, abuse or neglect
  - As a result of those care and support needs is unable to protect themselves from either the risk of, or the experience of, abuse or neglect
- All staff (employees, contractors, volunteers)
- All modes of delivery (online, blended, face-to-face)

## 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Safeguarding** | Protecting children and adults at risk from abuse, harm, and neglect; preventing impairment of health and development; and ensuring they grow up in circumstances consistent with the provision of safe and effective care |
| **Child** | Any person under the age of 18 (Children Act 1989) |
| **Adult at Risk** | A person aged 18 or over who may be in need of community care services by reason of mental or other disability, age, or illness, and who is or may be unable to take care of themselves or protect themselves against significant harm or exploitation |
| **DSL** | Designated Safeguarding Lead — the person with lead responsibility for safeguarding within the Centre |
| **Prevent Duty** | The statutory duty under section 26 of the Counter-Terrorism and Security Act 2015 to have due regard to the need to prevent people from being drawn into terrorism |
| **Radicalisation** | The process by which a person comes to support terrorism and extremist ideologies |
| **Disclosure** | When a person shares information about abuse or harm they are experiencing or at risk of |
| **Referral** | A formal notification to an external agency (e.g., social services, police) about a safeguarding concern |

## 4. Designated Safeguarding Lead (DSL)

The Centre appoints a **Designated Safeguarding Lead (DSL)** who holds overall responsibility for safeguarding. The DSL:

- Receives and responds to all safeguarding concerns and disclosures
- Maintains up-to-date knowledge of safeguarding legislation, guidance, and best practice
- Acts as the main point of contact with external agencies (law enforcement, social services, local safeguarding boards)
- Ensures all staff complete safeguarding induction and annual refresher training
- Maintains a confidential Safeguarding Log
- Reports on safeguarding to the Centre Director and governance meetings
- Reviews and updates this policy annually

A **Deputy DSL** is appointed to act in the DSL's absence. The Deputy DSL receives the same level of training and has the same responsibilities when acting in the role.

## 5. Categories of Abuse

All staff are trained to recognise potential indicators of the following categories of abuse:

### For Children and Adults

| Category | Potential Indicators |
|----------|---------------------|
| **Physical Abuse** | Unexplained injuries (bruises, burns, fractures); injuries inconsistent with the explanation given; flinching when approached; reluctance to seek medical help |
| **Emotional / Psychological Abuse** | Withdrawal, anxiety, depression; low self-esteem; changes in behaviour; fear of making mistakes; overly compliant or aggressive behaviour |
| **Sexual Abuse or Exploitation** | Age-inappropriate sexual behaviour or knowledge; physical signs (bruising, bleeding, infections); reluctance to undress or participate in physical activities; disclosure |
| **Neglect** | Persistent hunger, poor hygiene, inappropriate clothing; untreated medical conditions; frequent lateness or absence; abandonment |
| **Financial or Material Abuse** | Unexplained withdrawal of money; sudden inability to pay fees; missing possessions; pressure to sign financial documents (particularly relevant to adults at risk) |
| **Discriminatory Abuse** | Harassment, slurs, or exclusion based on a protected characteristic; unequal treatment; denial of rights |
| **Online / Digital Abuse** | Cyber-bullying, online grooming, sexting, exposure to harmful content; unauthorised sharing of images; coercive online behaviour |
| **Domestic Abuse** | Controlling, coercive, or threatening behaviour within an intimate or family relationship; includes psychological, physical, sexual, financial, and emotional abuse |
| **Self-Neglect** | Lack of self-care (hygiene, nutrition, health); hoarding; refusal of necessary support; neglect of one's environment |

### Radicalisation (Prevent Duty)

Indicators that a person may be vulnerable to radicalisation include:

- Expressing extremist or violent views
- Isolating themselves from family, friends, or usual social circles
- Accessing extremist materials online
- A marked change in behaviour, appearance, or peer group
- Use of extremist language or symbolism
- Expressing a desire or intent to travel to conflict zones

## 6. Responding to a Safeguarding Concern

If any staff member has a safeguarding concern about a learner, the following steps are taken:

### Step 1 — Immediate Safety
**If the learner is in immediate danger**, call 911 (USA emergency services) or the relevant local emergency number. Inform the DSL as soon as possible.

### Step 2 — Listen and Reassure
- Listen calmly, carefully, and without judgment.
- Do not ask leading questions. Use open questions only (e.g., "Can you tell me more about that?").
- Do not promise confidentiality. Explain that you may need to share the information to keep them or others safe.
- Reassure the person that they have done the right thing by telling you.
- Do not investigate independently or confront any alleged abuser.

### Step 3 — Record
Record what was said or observed **immediately**, using the Safeguarding Concern Form. The record should:
- Use the learner's own words where possible
- Note the date, time, place, and any witnesses
- Distinguish between fact, observation, and opinion
- Be signed and dated by the person making the record

### Step 4 — Report
Report the concern to the DSL **immediately** (within 24 hours at the latest). If the DSL is unavailable, report to the Deputy DSL or Centre Director. Do not delay.

### Step 5 — DSL Assessment and Action
The DSL assesses the concern and decides on the appropriate course of action. Options include:

| Action | When |
|--------|------|
| **No further action** | Concern does not meet the threshold for safeguarding intervention; recorded in Safeguarding Log with rationale |
| **Internal support and monitoring** | Low-level concern; the Centre provides support and monitors the situation |
| **Referral to external agency** | Concern meets the threshold for referral to social services, police, or other statutory agency. The DSL makes the referral within 24 hours (or immediately for urgent cases) |
| **Consultation with external agency** | DSL seeks informal advice from an external agency before deciding on next steps |

### Step 6 — Follow-Up
- The DSL follows up on referrals and keeps the referring staff member informed (on a need-to-know basis).
- The learner and, where appropriate, their family/carers are kept informed of the process.
- The Safeguarding Log is updated with all actions, decisions, and outcomes.

## 7. Online Safety

Given the Centre's online delivery model, specific online safeguarding measures are in place:

- **Approved Communication Channels:** All online interactions between staff and learners take place through Centre-approved channels (Moodle messaging, Centre email addresses, scheduled and recorded video sessions via approved platforms). Staff do not use personal social media accounts, personal phone numbers, or personal email addresses to communicate with learners.
- **No One-to-One Unrecorded Sessions:** Live one-to-one video sessions with learners under 18 or adults at risk are recorded (with consent) or conducted with a second staff member present.
- **Moodle Monitoring:** The Moodle platform is monitored for safeguarding risks, including harmful content, inappropriate communications, and signs of radicalisation.
- **Content Filtering:** Where technically feasible, content filtering is applied to Centre-provided systems.
- **Learner Guidance:** During induction, all learners receive guidance on:
  - Safe online behaviour
  - Protecting personal information
  - Recognising online grooming, cyber-bullying, and other risks
  - How to report online concerns
  - The Centre's Acceptable Use Policy for online platforms

## 8. Safer Recruitment

The Centre applies the following measures when recruiting all staff, contractors, and volunteers:

1. **Job Description and Person Specification** — all roles include reference to safeguarding responsibilities.
2. **Application Form** — requires a full employment history with explanations for any gaps.
3. **Identity Verification** — proof of identity and right to work in the relevant jurisdiction.
4. **Reference Checks** — a minimum of two professional referees, including the most recent employer. References are verified by telephone where possible. Specific safeguarding questions are included in reference requests.
5. **Background Checks** — criminal record checks as required by Georgia and US federal law. For roles involving regular contact with children or vulnerable adults, enhanced checks are sought.
6. **Interview** — includes questions that assess the candidate's understanding of and commitment to safeguarding.
7. **Qualification Verification** — original certificates are inspected and copies retained.
8. **Induction** — safeguarding is a mandatory component of induction for all new starters, completed before the person has unsupervised contact with learners.
9. **Probationary Review** — safeguarding compliance is assessed during the probationary period.

The Centre maintains a **Single Central Record** of all pre-employment checks.

## 9. Allegations Against Staff

If an allegation is made against a member of staff:

1. The allegation is reported immediately to the Centre Director (or the DSL, if the allegation involves the Centre Director).
2. The Centre Director assesses whether the allegation meets the threshold for referral to external agencies.
3. The staff member concerned may be suspended or have their duties adjusted while the matter is investigated. Suspension is a neutral act, not a presumption of guilt.
4. A formal investigation is conducted in accordance with the Centre's disciplinary procedure.
5. If the allegation is substantiated, the Centre takes appropriate disciplinary action, up to and including termination of contract, and notifies OTHM and any relevant professional or regulatory bodies.
6. If the allegation is not substantiated, the staff member is supported in returning to work and the record of the allegation is retained in accordance with the Document Storage and Retention Policy.

The Centre maintains a clear distinction between:
- An allegation that may meet the threshold for a criminal investigation (referred to police)
- An allegation that indicates the person poses a risk of harm (referred to social services or safeguarding board)
- A complaint or concern that is handled internally

## 10. Confidentiality and Information Sharing

- Safeguarding information is stored securely with access restricted to the DSL, Deputy DSL, and Centre Director.
- The principle of **"need to know"** governs all internal information sharing.
- Information may be shared with external agencies **without consent** where this is necessary to protect a person from harm. The DSL documents the rationale for any disclosure without consent.
- The Data Protection Act 2018 and UK GDPR do not prevent the sharing of information for safeguarding purposes. Safeguarding overrides data protection where there is a conflict.
- Staff are informed that any safeguarding records relating to them are retained in accordance with legal requirements, even after they leave the Centre.

## 12. Record Keeping

- The DSL maintains a confidential **Safeguarding Log** recording all concerns, assessments, actions, referrals, and outcomes.
- Each entry includes: date, time, source of concern, nature of concern, assessment, actions taken, outcome, and date closed.
- Supporting documents (Safeguarding Concern Forms, referral letters, correspondence) are filed securely.
- Safeguarding records are retained for a minimum of **7 years** (or longer if advised by legal counsel), even after the learner has left the Centre.
- Safeguarding records are stored separately from the learner's main academic file.
