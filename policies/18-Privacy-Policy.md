## 1. Introduction

ACLAS Global International Education Group ("the Centre", "we", "us", "our") is committed to protecting the privacy and security of personal data. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, store, share, and protect personal data in accordance with the UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR), the Data Protection Act 2018, and applicable US privacy laws including the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) where applicable.

This policy applies to all learners, prospective learners, staff, website visitors, and any other individuals whose personal data we process. It should be read alongside our Data Protection and GDPR Policy, which provides more detailed operational procedures for data handling.

## 6. Data Retention

We retain your personal data only for as long as is necessary for the purposes for which it was collected, or as required by law:

| Data Type | Retention Period |
|-----------|:----------------:|
| Learner academic records (submissions, grades, Assessor feedback) | 3 years after certification or withdrawal |
| Learner personal and contact data | 3 years after last interaction |
| Staff employment records | 3 years after termination |
| Financial and transaction records | 7 years (tax and accounting requirements) |
| Safeguarding records | 7 years minimum (legal guidance) |
| EQA reports and certification confirmations | Permanently |
| Website analytics and cookies | 26 months (Google Analytics default) |

At the end of the applicable retention period, personal data is securely destroyed. Physical documents are cross-shredded. Electronic records are permanently deleted from all systems, including backups. A disposal log is maintained for sensitive records.

## 7. Data Security

We implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect your personal data against unauthorised or unlawful processing, accidental loss, destruction, or damage:

- **Encryption:** Data in transit is encrypted using TLS 1.2 or higher. Data at rest is encrypted on secure servers using AES-256.
- **Access Controls:** Role-based access controls are implemented on Moodle and all internal systems. Staff access to personal data is restricted on a strict need-to-know basis.
- **Authentication:** Multi-factor authentication is enabled for all administrative accounts.
- **Network Security:** Firewalls, intrusion detection, and regular vulnerability scanning protect our infrastructure.
- **Backups:** Automated daily backups of all systems. Disaster recovery testing ensures restoration capability within 24 hours.
- **Staff Training:** All staff complete data protection training during induction and annually thereafter.
- **Physical Security:** Our physical office is secured with access control. Paper records are stored in locked filing cabinets.
- **Third-Party Management:** All third-party processors are vetted for security compliance and operate under a written Data Processing Agreement.

## 8. Your Data Protection Rights

Under the UK GDPR and Data Protection Act 2018, you have the following rights:

| Right | What It Means |
|-------|---------------|
| **Right to be informed** | You have the right to know how we use your personal data — this Privacy Policy serves that purpose |
| **Right of access** | You may request a copy of the personal data we hold about you (a Subject Access Request). This is free of charge and we will respond within 30 calendar days |
| **Right to rectification** | You may ask us to correct any inaccurate or incomplete personal data |
| **Right to erasure** | You may ask us to delete your personal data in certain circumstances (the "right to be forgotten"). This right is subject to legal retention requirements — for example, we must retain academic records for OTHM quality assurance |
| **Right to restrict processing** | You may ask us to limit how we use your data in certain circumstances (e.g., while we verify its accuracy) |
| **Right to data portability** | You may request your data in a structured, commonly used, machine-readable format, and have it transferred to another controller |
| **Right to object** | You may object to processing based on legitimate interests, including for direct marketing |
| **Rights relating to automated decision-making** | We do not use automated decision-making or profiling that produces legal effects concerning you |

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us at **privacy@aclas.global**. We will verify your identity before processing your request (to prevent unauthorised access). We will respond within 30 calendar days, or inform you if an extension is needed.

If you are dissatisfied with our response, you have the right to lodge a complaint with the **Information Commissioner's Office (ICO)** at www.ico.org.uk or by calling 0303 123 1113.

## 9. Cookies and Tracking Technologies

Our website (https://aclas.global) and Moodle platform use cookies and similar technologies. Cookies are small text files placed on your device that help us provide and improve our services.

| Cookie Category | Purpose | Examples |
|-----------------|---------|----------|
| **Strictly Necessary / Essential** | Required for the platform to function — user authentication, session management, security | Moodle session cookie (MoodleSession) |
| **Functional / Preference** | Remember your choices — language preferences, login details | Language preference cookie |
| **Analytics / Performance** | Understand how visitors use our website — pages visited, time on site, referring links | Google Analytics (_ga, _gid) |
| **Marketing** | Deliver relevant advertisements (only with consent) | Facebook Pixel (if used) |

You can manage your cookie preferences through:
- Our cookie consent banner (displayed on first visit)
- Your browser settings (most browsers allow you to block or delete cookies)
- Google's Ads Settings (for Google advertising cookies)

Please note that disabling essential cookies may prevent Moodle from functioning correctly.

## 12. Data Breach Notification

In the event of a personal data breach:

1. The breach is reported immediately to the Centre Director (Data Protection Officer).
2. We investigate promptly to determine the scope, cause, and impact.
3. We take immediate steps to contain and remediate the breach.
4. If the breach is likely to result in a risk to your rights and freedoms, we notify the **ICO within 72 hours** of becoming aware of it.
5. If the breach is likely to result in a high risk to your rights and freedoms, we notify you without undue delay.

All breaches are recorded in a Breach Register, including the facts, effects, and remedial action taken.
