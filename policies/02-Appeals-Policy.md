## 1. Purpose

This policy gives every learner at ACLAS Global International Education Group ("the Centre") a clear, fair, and timely route to challenge an academic decision that affects their progress or qualification outcome. The Centre is committed to handling every appeal impartially, without recrimination, and in accordance with the principles of natural justice.

This policy reflects and complies with OTHM's requirements for centre-approved appeals procedures and, where applicable, the expectations of the Office of the Independent Adjudicator (OIA) for Higher Education in the United Kingdom.

## 2. Scope

This policy covers appeals against:

- **Assessment decisions (unit grades):** Including Pass/Refer/Fail determinations made by an Assessor.
- **Decisions about reasonable adjustment requests:** Including refusal, partial grant, or the nature of the adjustment provided.
- **Decisions about Recognition of Prior Learning (RPL):** Including refusal to grant RPL, partial RPL award, or the conditions attached.
- **Findings of malpractice or plagiarism:** Including the finding itself, the sanction imposed, or both.
- **Decisions to withdraw a learner** from a programme on academic grounds.
- **Any other academic decision** that directly affects the learner's qualification outcome, progress, or certification.

**Exclusions:** Complaints about service quality, staff conduct, administrative matters, or the learning environment are handled under the separate **Complaints Policy**. Where a matter has elements of both an academic appeal and a non-academic complaint, the Centre Quality Lead determines the primary nature and routes it accordingly, ensuring both aspects are addressed.

## 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Appeal** | A formal request by a learner to review and overturn or vary an academic decision. |
| **Appellant** | The learner making the appeal. |
| **Impartial Reviewer** | A person appointed to consider the appeal who was not involved in the original decision. |
| **Remedy** | The corrective action taken if an appeal is upheld (e.g., grade change, reassessment opportunity, RPL granted). |
| **Natural Justice** | The principles that a decision-maker must act fairly, without bias, and give each party a reasonable opportunity to present their case. |
| **OTHM** | OTHM Qualifications, the awarding organisation. |
| **EQA** | External Quality Assurer appointed by OTHM. |

## 4. Grounds for Appeal

A learner may appeal on one or more of the following grounds only. Dissatisfaction with a grade, without one of these grounds, is not a valid appeal.

### Ground 1 — Procedural Irregularity
The assessment was not conducted in accordance with the published Assessment Policy, the relevant assignment brief, the OTHM qualification specification, or any other published Centre procedure, and this irregularity materially affected the outcome.

Examples include:
- The Assessor failed to follow the published marking scheme or assessment criteria.
- The learner was not provided with reasonable adjustments that had been formally agreed.
- The assessment was conducted outside the specified conditions or timeframes without proper authorisation.
- The Centre failed to follow its own IQA or standardisation procedures in a way that affected the decision.

### Ground 2 — Assessment Error
The Assessor made a factual error that materially affected the grade.

Examples include:
- Overlooking or failing to consider evidence that was submitted on time.
- Miscalculating marks or incorrectly totalling scores.
- Applying the wrong assessment criteria to the learner's work.
- Recording a grade that does not match the feedback and marksheet.

### Ground 3 — Unconsidered Extenuating Circumstances
There were significant personal circumstances that affected the learner's performance and that:
- The learner could not reasonably have been expected to disclose before the assessment decision was made, OR
- The learner did disclose but the Assessor or Centre did not adequately consider them.

Examples include:
- Serious illness, injury, or hospitalisation.
- Bereavement of a close family member or dependant.
- Significant personal trauma (e.g., victim of crime, domestic emergency).
- Other exceptional events beyond the learner's control.

**Note:** Circumstances known to the learner before the assessment deadline should have been raised at that time through the Centre's extenuating circumstances procedure. An appeal on this ground requires an explanation of why the circumstances were not raised earlier.

### Ground 4 — Bias or Discrimination
There is evidence that the assessment decision was influenced by bias, prejudice, or unlawful discrimination on the basis of a protected characteristic (as defined in the Equality Act 2010 — see the Centre's Diversity and Equality Policy).

## 5. Stage 1 — Informal Resolution

The Centre encourages learners to resolve concerns informally before lodging a formal appeal, as this is often faster and less stressful.

1. **Initiation:** Within **10 working days** of receiving the assessment decision, the learner contacts their Assessor in writing (email or Moodle message), explaining their concern and the outcome sought.
2. **Discussion:** The Assessor and learner may meet (in person, by video call, or by telephone) to discuss the concern. The learner may be accompanied by a peer supporter if they wish.
3. **Response:** The Assessor responds in writing within **5 working days**, explaining the original decision and either confirming it or proposing a resolution.
4. **Resolution:** If the learner accepts the resolution (e.g., the Assessor identifies an error and corrects it), the matter is closed and recorded.
5. **Escalation:** If the matter is not resolved, or if the learner prefers to proceed directly to a formal appeal, the learner may progress to Stage 2.

**Safeguard:** Engaging in Stage 1 does not affect the deadline for Stage 2. The Stage 2 deadline runs from the date of the Stage 1 outcome letter, not the original decision.

## 6. Stage 2 — Formal Appeal

### 6.1 Submission

1. The learner submits a formal written appeal to **appeals@aclas.global** within **10 working days** of:
   - The Stage 1 outcome, or
   - The original assessment decision, if Stage 1 was bypassed.

2. The appeal submission must include:
   - **Learner identification:** Full name, OTHM Learner Registration Number, contact email.
   - **Decision appealed:** The qualification, unit title, assessment task, and date of the decision.
   - **Ground(s) of appeal:** A clear statement of which ground(s) from Section 4 are relied upon, with an explanation of how each ground applies.
   - **Supporting evidence:** All documents the learner wishes to be considered (e.g., the original submission, the Assessor's feedback, correspondence, medical evidence, witness statements).
   - **Outcome sought:** A clear statement of the remedy the learner is seeking (e.g., grade changed to Pass, resubmission opportunity, reassessment by a different Assessor).
   - **Stage 1 outcome (if applicable):** A copy of the Stage 1 response letter, if Stage 1 was attempted.

3. The Centre provides an **Appeals Submission Form** to assist learners in structuring their appeal. Use of the form is recommended but not mandatory.

### 6.2 Acknowledgement and Initial Review

1. The Centre Quality Lead acknowledges receipt within **3 working days**.
2. The Centre Quality Lead conducts a preliminary review to confirm:
   - The appeal is within scope (Section 2).
   - At least one valid ground has been identified (Section 4).
   - The appeal is submitted within the time limit (or an extension has been granted).
3. If the appeal does not meet these criteria, the Centre Quality Lead notifies the learner in writing within **5 working days**, explaining why the appeal cannot proceed and the learner's right to escalate to OTHM.

### 6.3 Appointment of Impartial Reviewer

1. The Centre Quality Lead appoints an **Impartial Reviewer** who:
   - Was **not** the original Assessor for the unit in question.
   - Was **not** the IQA who sampled the unit in question.
   - Has appropriate subject knowledge to evaluate the assessment evidence.
   - Has no personal, professional, or financial relationship with the appellant that could give rise to bias.

2. Where the Centre's size makes complete independence difficult, the Centre Quality Lead documents the steps taken to maximise impartiality and, if necessary, requests an external reviewer or declares the arrangement to OTHM.

3. The appellant is notified of the reviewer's identity and may object within **3 working days** if they believe the reviewer is not impartial.

### 6.4 Investigation

The Impartial Reviewer:

1. Examines all documents, including:
   - The original assessment submission.
   - The Assessor's feedback, marksheet, and any Unit Assessment Record.
   - The assignment brief and marking criteria.
   - All relevant IQA records for the unit.
   - Any records of reasonable adjustments in place.
   - The appellant's appeal submission and all supporting evidence.
   - The Stage 1 record (if applicable).

2. May, at their discretion:
   - Interview the appellant (the appellant may be accompanied by a peer supporter).
   - Interview the Assessor.
   - Interview the Lead IQA or any other relevant staff member.
   - Request additional information or clarification from either party.
   - Seek an independent subject-matter opinion where specialist knowledge is required.

3. Conducts the investigation without undue delay, aiming to complete it within **15 working days** of appointment. If more time is needed, the appellant is notified of the delay and the revised timeline.

### 6.5 Decision

1. The Impartial Reviewer issues a written decision within **15 working days** of appointment. The decision letter:
   - Summarises the grounds raised by the appellant.
   - Sets out the reviewer's findings on each ground, with reasons.
   - States whether the appeal is **upheld**, **partially upheld**, or **dismissed**.
   - If upheld or partially upheld, specifies the **remedy** (e.g., grade changed, resubmission with no penalty, reassessment by a different Assessor, RPL granted, reasonable adjustment approved, malpractice finding overturned or sanction reduced).
   - States the deadline by which the remedy will be implemented.
   - Explains the learner's right to escalate to OTHM (Stage 3) and the timeframe for doing so.

2. The decision is final at the Centre level. The Centre implements the remedy promptly and notifies the appellant when it has been done.

3. A copy of the decision is placed on the learner's file and the appeal register.

## 7. Stage 3 — Escalation to OTHM

If the learner remains dissatisfied after Stage 2, they may escalate the appeal to OTHMQualifications.

1. The learner must notify the Centre of their intention to escalate within **10 working days** of receiving the Stage 2 decision. Notification is sent to **appeals@aclas.global**.

2. The Centre forwards the complete appeal file to OTHM within **5 working days** of receiving the learner's notification. The file includes:
   - The learner's original appeal submission and all evidence.
   - The Stage 1 record (if any).
   - The Impartial Reviewer's decision letter.
   - The Centre's Appeal Register entry for this case.
   - Any other documents requested by OTHM.

3. OTHM conducts its review in accordance with its own procedures. OTHM's decision is final and binding on both the Centre and the learner.

4. The Centre implements any remedy directed by OTHM within the timeframe specified.

## 8. Time Extensions

- A learner who needs more time to submit an appeal due to illness, disability, or other compelling circumstances may request an extension from the Centre Quality Lead.
- The request must be made before the applicable deadline expires.
- Extensions are granted at the Centre Quality Lead's discretion. Extensions are normally for a specified period (e.g., an additional 10 working days).
- The Centre Quality Lead may also extend investigation timelines where additional time is genuinely needed to gather evidence or when key personnel are unavailable.

## 9. Status of Learner During Appeal

- A learner who has lodged an appeal remains enrolled and continues to have access to Moodle and Tutor support for their current units, unless the appeal relates to a finding of serious malpractice that warrants immediate suspension (see Malpractice and Plagiarism Policy).
- Where the appeal concerns a unit that is a prerequisite for further study, the Centre Quality Lead decides whether the learner may progress provisionally pending the appeal outcome.
- Certification for the qualification as a whole is withheld until the appeal is resolved.

## 10. Record Keeping

The Centre maintains a confidential **Appeal Register** recording every formal appeal. For each case, the following records are retained for a minimum of **3 years** after resolution:

- The learner's appeal submission and all supporting evidence.
- Stage 1 correspondence and outcome (if applicable).
- The Impartial Reviewer's appointment record.
- Investigation notes and records of any interviews.
- The formal Stage 2 decision letter.
- Records of any remedy implemented.
- Stage 3 escalation records (if applicable).
- Confirmation that the learner was informed of the final outcome and any further escalation rights.

Records are stored securely in accordance with the **Data Protection and GDPR Policy** and the **Document Storage and Retention Policy**.

## 11. Confidentiality and Non-Retaliation

- All information relating to an appeal is treated confidentially and shared only with those who need it for the purposes of investigation and resolution.
- The Centre prohibits any form of retaliation or victimisation against a learner for making an appeal in good faith. Any such conduct is treated as a disciplinary matter and may constitute a breach of the **Diversity and Equality Policy**.
- A learner who believes they have been subjected to retaliation may raise the matter through the **Complaints Policy**.

## 12. Monitoring and Continuous Improvement

1. The Centre Quality Lead conducts an **annual review** of all appeals recorded in the Appeal Register.
2. The review analyses:
   - Number and type of appeals received.
   - Grounds cited and outcomes (upheld / partially upheld / dismissed).
   - Patterns by qualification, unit, Assessor, or learner characteristic.
   - Average time to resolution at each stage.
   - Themes in OTHM escalation outcomes.

3. Findings are reported to the Centre Director and feed into:
   - The **Annual Self-Assessment Report** (Quality Assurance Policy).
   - **Standardisation meetings** (trends discussed with Assessors).
   - **CPD planning** (targeted training where patterns of assessment error are identified).
   - **IQA strategy review** (adjusting sampling rates where appeal patterns emerge).
