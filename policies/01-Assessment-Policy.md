## 1. Purpose

This policy defines how ACLAS Global International Education Group ("the Centre") designs, conducts, marks, and quality-assures assessments for all OTHM qualifications. Its purpose is to ensure that every assessment decision is:

- **Valid** — measuring the intended learning outcomes
- **Reliable** — consistent across Assessors and over time
- **Fair** — free from bias and accessible to all learners
- **Rigorous** — upholding the standards of the qualification

This policy operates within the framework of the Centre's overarching Quality Assurance Policy and is supplemented by the Internal Quality Assurance (IQA) Policy, the Malpractice and Plagiarism Policy, and the Reasonable Adjustment Policy.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:

- All OTHM qualification programmes delivered by the Centre through any mode — online, blended, or face-to-face
- All staff who teach, assess, internally quality-assure, or manage learner work
- All learners from the point of enrolment through to certification
- All assessment methods used: written assignments, portfolios, professional discussions, presentations, reflective journals, practical tasks, and any other method specified in OTHM qualification specifications

## 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Formative Assessment** | Assessment conducted during the learning process to provide developmental feedback and monitor progress. It does not contribute to the final grade but informs both the learner and Tutor of areas needing improvement. |
| **Summative Assessment** | Assessment conducted at the end of a unit or programme to measure the learner's achievement against specified assessment criteria. Summative decisions determine whether a unit is passed or referred. |
| **Assessment Criteria** | The specific, measurable statements published in the OTHM qualification specification that define what a learner must demonstrate to achieve each learning outcome. |
| **Learning Outcome** | A statement of what a learner is expected to know, understand, or be able to do upon successful completion of a unit. |
| **Assessment Brief** | A document setting out the task(s) a learner must complete, together with the relevant learning outcomes, assessment criteria, submission requirements, and deadline. |
| **Pass** | The grade awarded when the evidence presented meets all the assessment criteria for the unit. |
| **Refer** | The decision that the evidence does not yet meet one or more assessment criteria. The learner is given the opportunity to resubmit after addressing the gaps identified in Assessor feedback. |
| **Fail** | The decision recorded when a learner has exhausted all permitted attempts without meeting the criteria, or where no further attempts are permitted under the Centre's rules. |
| **Assessor** | A qualified member of staff responsible for marking learner work against assessment criteria and providing feedback. |
| **Marking Scheme / Unit Assessment Record** | The standardised document on which the Assessor records for each assessment criterion whether the evidence is sufficient, the feedback provided, and the overall unit grade. |
| **Scheme of Work (SoW)** | A planning document that maps out how each unit will be taught and assessed across the delivery period, including session topics, activities, resources, and assessment points. |

## 4. Assessment Principles

Every assessment produced or graded by the Centre must satisfy the following six principles, which are derived from OTHM's Centre Approval Criteria and regulatory expectations of Ofqual-recognised awarding organisations:

### 4.1 Validity
The assessment task must genuinely measure the learning outcomes and assessment criteria it claims to assess. The Centre ensures validity by:
- Aligning every assessment task to specific learning outcomes and assessment criteria
- Using OTHM-authorised Assignment Briefs wherever available
- Having internally developed briefs approved by the Lead IQA and, where required, by OTHM before issue to learners

### 4.2 Reliability
Different Assessors, marking the same evidence against the same criteria, should reach the same decision. Reliability is assured through:
- Standardised marking using Unit Assessment Records
- Regular standardisation meetings (minimum quarterly)
- Cross-marking exercises during standardisation
- IQA sampling across all Assessors

### 4.3 Fairness
No learner is advantaged or disadvantaged by factors unrelated to the assessment criteria. Fairness is ensured through:
- Clear, published assessment criteria known to learners before they begin work
- Reasonable adjustments provided in accordance with the Reasonable Adjustment Policy
- Consistent application of the Centre's Malpractice and Plagiarism Policy
- Avoidance of conflicts of interest (see Conflicts of Interest Policy)

### 4.4 Transparency
Before assessment begins, every learner receives:
- The Assignment Brief containing the task(s), learning outcomes, assessment criteria, word count or equivalent, and submission deadline
- An explanation of how feedback will be given and when
- Information about resubmission rights and procedures
- Guidance on academic integrity, referencing, and the acceptable use of AI

### 4.5 Authenticity
The Centre takes active steps to verify that assessed work is the learner's own:
- All written submissions are submitted through the Centre's Moodle LMS, which integrates plagiarism-detection software
- Assessors are trained to identify indicators of contract cheating, collusion, impersonation, and unauthorised AI use
- The Centre may require learners to complete a viva-style professional discussion to confirm the authenticity of submitted work
- Suspected malpractice is handled under the Malpractice and Plagiarism Policy

### 4.6 Sufficiency
The evidence presented must be enough to demonstrate consistent achievement across all learning outcomes. Assessors consider:
- Coverage: Does the evidence address every learning outcome and assessment criterion?
- Quality: Is the evidence at the level expected for the qualification?
- Currency: Is the evidence recent enough to demonstrate current competence?
- Authenticity: Is the evidence the learner's own work?

## 5. Assessment Methods

The Centre uses assessment methods appropriate to the level, subject, and mode of delivery. The choice of method for each unit is recorded in the Scheme of Work and Assignment Brief.

| Method | Description | Typical Use |
|--------|-------------|-------------|
| **Written Assignment** | Extended essay, report, case-study analysis, or research paper | Most OTHM units at Levels 3–7 |
| **Portfolio of Evidence** | Compilation of work produced over the course of a unit, demonstrating progressive achievement | Units requiring sustained practical or reflective work |
| **Professional Discussion** | Structured, recorded conversation between Assessor and learner exploring knowledge, understanding, and application | Verifying authenticity; assessing applied knowledge |
| **Presentation** | Live online (via video conferencing) or pre-recorded presentation with supporting slides and speaker notes | Units requiring communication and presentation skills |
| **Reflective Journal / Learning Log** | Ongoing record of learning experiences, reflections, and development | Personal and professional development units |
| **Practical Task / Simulation** | A task designed to replicate a real-world scenario relevant to the qualification | Units with practical learning outcomes |

All recorded professional discussions and presentations are retained as assessment evidence in accordance with the Document Storage and Retention Policy.

## 6. Formative Assessment

Formative assessment is integral to the Centre's teaching and learning approach. It provides learners with feedback during the learning process before summative assessment takes place.

### 6.1 Methods
- Self-assessment quizzes on Moodle with automated feedback
- Draft assignment review (Assessor provides indicative feedback on one draft section per unit)
- Discussion forum participation
- In-session Q&A and practice exercises
- Peer review activities in group cohorts

### 6.2 Principles
- Formative feedback is developmental, not judgmental
- It identifies strengths and gaps without assigning a pass/refer grade
- It is provided within 10 working days (for draft review)
- It is not binding on the final summative decision

## 7. Summative Assessment — Assignment Briefs

### 7.1 Authorised Briefs
The Centre uses OTHM-authorised Assignment Briefs wherever they are available. These briefs have been approved by OTHM as meeting the requirements of the relevant qualification specification.

### 7.2 Centre-Developed Briefs
Where the Centre develops its own Assignment Brief:
1. The Assessor drafts the brief, ensuring every task maps to specific learning outcomes and assessment criteria
2. The Lead IQA reviews the brief for validity, fairness, and clarity
3. The brief is submitted to OTHM for approval (via email to the designated OTHM contact) before it is issued to learners
4. The Centre does not issue a centre-developed brief to learners until OTHM approval is confirmed in writing
5. The approved brief, together with a record of OTHM's approval, is retained in accordance with the Document Storage and Retention Policy

### 7.3 Content of Every Assignment Brief
Every Assignment Brief, whether OTHM-authorised or centre-developed, must clearly state:
- Unit title and OTHM unit code
- Qualification title and level
- All learning outcomes and assessment criteria for the unit
- The task(s) the learner must complete
- Word count or equivalent (e.g., presentation duration) for each task
- Submission deadline (date and time)
- Submission method (via Moodle)
- Grading criteria — what constitutes a Pass
- Referencing and academic integrity requirements
- The Centre's policy on AI use (summarised)
- How and when feedback will be provided
- Information about resubmission rights

## 8. Assessment Planning

### 8.1 Individual Assessment Plan
- At the start of their programme, each learner receives an Individual Assessment Plan listing:
  - Every unit to be assessed
  - The assessment method for each unit
  - Submission windows (dates by which the learner should aim to submit)
  - Any agreed reasonable adjustments
- The plan is reviewed at least once per term (or every 12 weeks for self-paced learners) and updated if circumstances change
- Learners receive a copy via Moodle and can request a review at any time

### 8.2 Scheme of Work
- Tutors prepare a Scheme of Work for each unit, mapping lesson topics, learning activities, resources, formative assessments, and summative assessment points across the delivery period
- The Scheme of Work is reviewed and approved by the Lead IQA before the unit commences
- It is retained as a quality record

## 9. Marking and Grading

### 9.1 Grade Scale
- **Pass:** The learner has provided sufficient evidence to meet all learning outcomes and assessment criteria
- **Refer:** The learner has not yet met one or more assessment criteria and is entitled to one resubmission opportunity
- **Fail:** The learner has exhausted all permitted attempts without meeting the criteria, or the case has been escalated under the Malpractice and Plagiarism Policy

### 9.2 Marking Process
1. The learner submits their work via the designated submission portal on Moodle by the published deadline
2. The submission is automatically checked by plagiarism-detection software
3. The Assessor reviews the work against every assessment criterion, completing the Unit Assessment Record
4. For each criterion, the Assessor records:
   - Whether the evidence is sufficient (met / not met)
   - Specific comments referencing the evidence submitted
   - Where a criterion is not met, a clear explanation of what is missing or insufficient
5. The Assessor records the overall unit grade (Pass or Refer)
6. The Assessor provides general developmental feedback (see Section 10)
7. The Assessor signs and dates the Unit Assessment Record
8. The marked work, together with the Unit Assessment Record, is made available to the learner via Moodle within 15 working days

### 9.3 Turnaround Time
- Summative assessment feedback is provided within **15 working days** of the submission deadline
- Where this deadline cannot be met (e.g., due to Assessor illness), the Centre Quality Lead notifies learners of the revised date and the reason
- Extended turnaround times are recorded and reviewed as part of the Centre's quality monitoring

## 10. Feedback to Learners

### 10.1 Summative Feedback
For every summative assessment, the Assessor provides written feedback that:
- Identifies what the learner did well, with specific reference to the evidence
- Identifies which criteria have not been met (if any) and why
- Provides specific, actionable guidance on what the learner needs to do to meet the outstanding criteria
- Is constructive, respectful, and free from discriminatory or pejorative language
- Is recorded on the Unit Assessment Record or as a separate feedback document

### 10.2 Refer Feedback
Where a learner receives a Refer decision:
- The feedback explains precisely which assessment criteria have not been met
- It describes the gap between the evidence provided and the standard required
- It identifies specific actions the learner should take to address the gap
- It sets out the resubmission deadline and any conditions (see Section 11)

### 10.3 Follow-Up
- Learners may request a follow-up discussion (by video call or telephone) with their Assessor within 5 working days of receiving feedback
- The Assessor responds within 3 working days to arrange the discussion
- Significant points raised during the discussion are noted and retained

## 11. Resubmission

### 11.1 Entitlement
- A learner who receives a Refer decision on first submission is entitled to **one resubmission opportunity per unit at no additional charge**
- The resubmission must be submitted within an agreed timeframe — normally no fewer than **10 working days** and no more than **20 working days** after feedback is received, unless an extension is approved by the Centre Quality Lead

### 11.2 Resubmission Process
1. The Assessor specifies the resubmission deadline and any conditions (e.g., which sections must be reworked) in the feedback
2. The learner resubmits the work via Moodle, addressing the gaps identified
3. The Assessor re-marks the work against all assessment criteria — not only the criteria previously not met
4. If all criteria are now met, the grade is recorded as Pass
5. If criteria remain unmet, the Assessor refers the case to the Centre Quality Lead

### 11.3 Further Attempts
- The Centre normally permits a maximum of **two attempts per unit** (first submission + one resubmission)
- A third and final attempt may be granted at the discretion of the Centre Quality Lead, only where there are compelling mitigating circumstances supported by evidence
- A fee may apply for a third attempt (see Refund Policy for fee details)
- Where a learner fails after all permitted attempts, the Centre Quality Lead reviews the case and may recommend that the learner:
  - Re-enrol on the unit (paying the unit fee again)
  - Transfer to a different qualification more suited to their capabilities
  - Withdraw from the programme

## 12. Late Submission

### 12.1 Expectations
Learners are expected to meet published deadlines. Deadlines are set at the start of the unit and communicated through the Assignment Brief and Moodle calendar.

### 12.2 Extension Requests
- A learner who needs an extension must request it **in writing before the deadline**, stating the reason and providing supporting evidence where relevant (e.g., medical certificate)
- Extension requests are submitted to the Assessor via Moodle or email
- The Assessor may grant an extension of **up to 10 working days** without further approval
- Extensions beyond 10 working days require approval from the Centre Quality Lead
- The decision is communicated to the learner in writing within 3 working days of the request

### 12.3 Late Submission Without Approval
- Late submission without an approved extension is accepted but may be capped at a Pass grade with no further resubmission opportunity, at the discretion of the Centre Quality Lead
- Work submitted more than 20 working days late without an approved extension may not be accepted, and the learner may be required to re-enrol on the unit
- Persistent late submission is escalated to the Centre Quality Lead for review

## 13. Academic Integrity and the Use of Artificial Intelligence

### 13.1 Plagiarism Detection
- All written submissions are run through plagiarism-detection software integrated with the Centre's Moodle LMS
- The software generates a similarity report that the Assessor reviews as part of the marking process
- A high similarity score is not, by itself, proof of plagiarism; the Assessor exercises professional judgment in interpreting the report

### 13.2 Use of Artificial Intelligence (AI)
In line with the OTHM Guidance Statement to Centres on the Risk of AI:

**Permitted Use:**
- AI tools may be used for research assistance (e.g., summarising background reading), provided sources are verified and acknowledged
- AI tools may be used for language editing, grammar checking, and improving clarity
- AI tools may be used for brainstorming and structuring ideas, provided the substantive intellectual work is the learner's own

**Prohibited Use:**
- Learners must not use AI tools to generate complete or substantive assessment responses and submit them as their own work
- Learners must not use AI tools to paraphrase source material in a way that obscures the original source or avoids genuine understanding
- Learners must not use AI tools to generate references, data, or quotations that they have not verified

**Declaration:**
- Every learner must include an AI Use Declaration with each assessment submission, stating:
  - Whether AI tools were used
  - Which tools were used and for what purpose
  - How the learner ensured the final work represents their own understanding
- Failure to declare AI use where it is subsequently detected may constitute malpractice

### 13.3 Contract Cheating and Impersonation
- The Centre is alert to the risk of contract cheating (paying a third party to complete assessment work) and impersonation
- Indicators include: writing style inconsistent with the learner's other work, use of vocabulary or concepts beyond the learner's demonstrated level, metadata anomalies
- The Centre may, at its discretion, require a learner to complete a supplementary assessment (e.g., a professional discussion) to verify authenticity
- All suspected cases are handled under the Malpractice and Plagiarism Policy

## 14. Assessor Allocation

- Each learner is assigned a named Assessor at the start of their programme
- The Centre maintains an Assessor-to-learner ratio that allows each learner to receive timely, individualised feedback
- No Assessor marks the work of a close relative, partner, or person with whom they have a financial or business relationship (see Conflicts of Interest Policy)
- An Assessor who has a conflict of interest (or perceives a potential conflict) must declare it and seek reassignment
- Where a learner requests a change of Assessor (for reasons of perceived bias or breakdown of the working relationship), the Centre Quality Lead considers the request and, where appropriate, arranges a reassignment

## 16. Internal Quality Assurance of Assessment

All assessment decisions are subject to Internal Quality Assurance (IQA) before they are submitted to OTHM for External Quality Assurance (EQA). The IQA process is governed by the separate IQA Policy. In summary:

- The Lead IQA samples assessment decisions across all Assessors, units, learners, and grade levels according to a risk-based sampling plan
- Sampling takes place **before** the unit is submitted for EQA
- Where IQA identifies an issue, the Assessor addresses it before the unit proceeds to EQA
- IQA findings feed into standardisation meetings, Assessor CPD, and the annual self-assessment
- The IQA log is maintained in real time and is available for EQA review

### 16.1 Interim vs Summative IQA
- **Interim IQA:** Conducted during the assessment process for a unit, providing formative feedback to the Assessor on their marking and feedback quality. It allows issues to be corrected early.
- **Summative IQA:** Conducted after all assessments for a unit have been marked and before EQA submission. It confirms that assessment decisions across the cohort are valid, reliable, and consistent.

Both types are recorded in the IQA log.

## 17. Standardisation

### 17.1 Schedule
The Centre holds a standardisation meeting at least **once per quarter**, and additionally whenever:
- A new qualification is introduced
- A new Assessor joins the Centre
- EQA feedback identifies systemic issues
- Significant changes are made to OTHM qualification specifications

### 17.2 Activities
- Cross-marking of anonymised learner work by two or more Assessors, comparing decisions and discussing differences
- Review of borderline Pass/Refer cases
- Discussion of EQA findings and action points
- Sharing of good practice in feedback writing
- Calibration of expectations for different qualification levels

### 17.3 Records
Minutes of every standardisation meeting are recorded and retained. They include:
- Date, attendees, and agenda
- Summary of discussions and decisions
- Any action points with owners and deadlines
- Follow-up on actions from the previous meeting

## 19. Monitoring and Review

### 19.1 Monitoring
The Centre Quality Lead monitors assessment practice through:
- Review of IQA reports and the IQA log
- Analysis of grade distribution across Assessors and units
- Review of assessment turnaround times
- Learner feedback from course evaluations and complaints
- EQA reports and action points

### 19.2 Annual Review
This policy is reviewed annually by the Centre Quality Lead in consultation with the Lead IQA, Assessors, and the Centre Director. The review considers:
- Changes to OTHM requirements or qualification specifications
- Findings from IQA and EQA activities
- Learner feedback and appeals data
- Developments in educational assessment practice
- Changes in legislation (e.g., data protection, equality)
